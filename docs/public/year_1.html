FIRST YEAR

ALGEBRA I

Introduction to the fundamentals of linear algebra, covering topics such as matrices, determinants, systems of linear equations, and basic operations with vectors in Euclidean space.

ALGEBRA II

Continuation of Algebra I, with an emphasis on more advanced algebraic structures, such as vector spaces, linear transformations, eigenvalues, and eigenvectors.

CALCULUS I

Study of differential and integral calculus in one variable, including limits, derivatives, differentiation rules, applications of the derivative, and integration techniques.

CALCULUS II

Extension of Calculus I to multivariable calculus, addressing concepts such as partial derivatives, gradient, multiple integrals, and applications to real-world problems.

PROGRAMMING I

Introduction to programming logic and algorithm design, using a basic programming language to solve computational problems.

LOGIC AND CRITICAL THINKING

Development of logical reasoning, argumentation, and critical analysis skills, with applications to solving mathematical and scientific problems.

KNOWLEDGE MANAGEMENT AND PERSONAL SKILLS

Course focused on personal and professional development, promoting time management, communication, and teamwork skills.

DISCRETE MATHEMATICS

Study of discrete mathematical structures such as sets, relations, graphs, and combinatorics, essential for computer science.

MATHEMATICAL AND COMPUTATIONAL LOGIC

Exploration of the principles of propositional logic and predicate logic, with applications in circuit design and algorithms.

HISTORY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY

Overview of the most important milestones in the development of science and technology, with an emphasis on their social and ethical implications.

INTEGRATIVE PROJECT I

Execution of an interdisciplinary project that allows the application of knowledge acquired in first-year courses.

FUNDAMENTALS OF COMPUTER ENGINEERING

Introduction to the basic principles of computer engineering, including the design of computational systems and analysis of their operation.

ELECTRONICS AND COMPUTER TECHNOLOGY

Study of basic electronic components and their application in the design and construction of hardware systems.

FIFTH YEAR
FUNDAMENTALS OF COMPUTER ENGINEERING
CONTENT

PART I: THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS

    BLOCK 1. Computer Engineering in Society

    • Historical background and perspectives. Definitions and basic concepts. Past, present, and future of ICT.
    • Computer Engineering as an academic discipline and profession. Required skills and knowledge for computer engineers. Professional opportunities. The role of ethics in computer engineering education. Professional codes of conduct.

    BLOCK 2. Current Paradigms and Trends in Systems and Applications

    • Software development: more than just programming.
    • Structure of computer systems: hardware, software, and networks.
    • New paradigms and trends: cloud computing, quantum computing, cognitive computing, blockchain, smart cities, IoT...

    BLOCK 3. Information and Data

    • Representation of information in digital format: number systems, numerical and alphanumeric representation (codes).
    • Data organization. Data models. Big Data challenges. Data protection.

PART II: PRACTICAL SKILLS

    BLOCK 1. Introduction to Software Development

    • Introduction to web development.

    BLOCK 2. Creating HTML5 Web Pages

    • Basic concepts. Tags. Information organization and layout with CSS3.

    BLOCK 3. Design and Layout with CSS3

    • Basic concepts. Selectors. Box model. Element positioning.
OBJECTIVES
  • Understand the purpose of Computer Engineering, its ultimate goal, and the benefit it brings to society, reflecting on the role each person wants to play as an engineer in the future society.
  • Know the pillars that should underpin technological progress, having an initial historical perspective on technological development.
  • Become aware of the importance of the human factor in the professional practice, encouraging the vocation of service and the sense of contribution to the common good.
  • Gain a global vision of the career, its different areas of knowledge, and distinguish professional opportunities and profiles to begin questioning one's vocation.
  • Provide knowledge on current paradigms, computing trends, data management, and an introduction to web development.
LEARNING OUTCOMES
  • Recognize and use, in an explanation or discourse, both hardware and software terminology involved in the creation, management, and operation of a computer system, related to any field of knowledge in computer engineering.
  • Describe and relate key historical events in the emergence and evolution of computers and computer science, as well as present and analyze future trends.
  • Explain the fundamentals and apply the various mechanisms of representing and organizing both numerical and non-numerical information within a computer system.
  • Explain the basic aspects of the fundamental areas of Computer Engineering: hardware, software, and networks.
  • Create a web system for the dissemination of information.
  • Research and properly present a computer project, both orally and in writing.
